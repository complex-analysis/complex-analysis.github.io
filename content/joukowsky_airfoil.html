Joukowsky Airfoil

Consider the uniform flow past a circular cylinder with circulation $C$ and speed $U>0$ given by the complex potential \begin{eqnarray}\label{eq1} F(z)=Uz+\frac{Ua^2}{z}-\frac{i C}{2\pi}\log z. \end{eqnarray} The stream function is just the imaginary part of this (\ref{eq1}), namely \begin{eqnarray}\label{eq2} \psi = Uy\left(1-\frac{a^2}{x^2+y^2}\right)-\frac{C}{4\pi}\log\left(x^2+y^2\right). \end{eqnarray} Hence the circle $x^2+y^2=a^2$ is still a streamline, with $\psi = -(C/2\pi)\log a$.

Exercise: Find the real part component of the complex potential defined in (\ref{eq1}). The imaginary component is already given, try to find it in order to confirm expression (\ref{eq2}).

Simulation

Consider now the uniform flow past the circular cylinder $c_0$ for $a=1$. We use the linear transformation $T(z)=-0.15+0.23i +r z$ to map the flow with circulation $C$ and speed $U$ around $|z|=1$ onto the flow around the circle $c_1$ with center $z_1=-0.15+0.23i$ and radius $r=0.23\sqrt{13\cdot 2}$. Then we use the mapping \[ J(z)=z+\frac{1}{z} \] to map this flow around the Joukowsky airfoil.
